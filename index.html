1 May 1856

Today is the frist full day that we have been in Graton. The men got to working on getting brick and wood for our homes and getting ready to plant the trees and wheat. The children all had fun playing Duck on the Rock. Having Brother Tenney

5-30 April 1856

It as been a short three weeks as we said good by to the people that we love here in Virgin. We are exited to meet the rest of the familes that will be coming with us to Grafton. They all seem like nice familes, Annie Shrits looks good just for having a baby a month ago. The Bareny's and Davies love all the children. The Barenys never had children and the Davies lost all of their children on the trek west. We found out that Grafton will be growing cotton and wheat. Frank Platt and Edward Shrits were ask to be in change of the planting and harvest. Jack Davies was a teacher in Denmark and as been ask to teach the children. On April 30 we headed out to Grafton with Brother Tenney he as been ask to take us to Grafton. He will also be helping planting the cotton trees and wheat.

4 April 1856

I am Nancy, I am married to the love of my life Willie. We were called to settle a town by the name of Grafton. It is about 1 mile away from Virgin,Ut. We live in Virgin and were are waiting for the rest of the familes to get to Vrigin before we headout to Grafton. Which will be by the end of the month. Willie as a print shop here in Vrigin and will come into Vrigin daily to work so he can still keep it open. We are happy and exited that Frank and Emma Platt are coming.Our children are really good friends with their grandchildren. Frank is a doctor and his office is in Vrigin by Willie's office. Willie and Frank decidce that they will go to Vrigin together so that they can leave a buggy with us in the case we need it. I want to write down as much as I can about our time in Grafton. Willie thinks that is a good idea for me to do that way when we are gone people will know about the frist familes in Grafton.
