Grafton Times

30 June 1859

The men got the ditch made so that when it rains the water won't go toward the homes and school. Willie as been doing his best to help when he can when he is not at his office. WHen Willie came home today he told us that scarlet fever was going a round and that we all need to stay in Grafton.

16 June 1859

Today it rained and it rained hard. We are glad that we got the plants into the grown before it stated. It rained to the point that it flooded. We decide that we need a way for the water not get too close to our homes. tomorrow will be the first day Willie will not be home all day. He is going to his office twice a week so he is closer to his clients. We will miss him when he is gone all day. He made sure he helped as much as he can when he can.

15 June 1859

It as been a busy couple of days but all the homes and school house are done. We have been working on the wheat fields and marking where to put it. Jack Davies and Frank Platt decide that it may be too warm to plant the wheat now for it to be a good crop. They did decide to plant the cotton trees and bushes.

1 June 1859

yesterday was a long day! We left later then we wanted to for Virgin and got to Grafton just before sunset. Today we got working on building our homes and get started on getting the fields ready to start planting. The men are hard at work when Willie had to run into Virgin because Timothy Lee came and said that his daughter fall out of the loft and was in a lot of pain. Willie hurry and got cleaned up and went to The Lee's home. The other men decide to get our home done first and to add a sick house for Willie can bring people closer to him. Willie got home after dark and said that the Lee's daughter broke her arm and leg but should be fine. He was so surprise that our home was done and When he was told about the sick house he was grateful for it and said that he will use it. Our home is the only one that got done today and tomorrow the men will get working on the rest of the homes. Willie said that he wants the homes done before the sick house. Which all the men said that was a great idea as it soon will be the rainy season.

21-30 May 1859

We have been busy the last coupe of days getting ready to move. Willie cleaned up is office to get it ready for him to be gone for a week as Richard got land to open a blacksmith shop. We also make sure we are stock up on everything that we need(tools,seed,bullets and more). We end off on the 31st to be in Grafton by June 1st.

20 May 1859

Today we got to meet the Shirts and Barneys families as well as Brother Tenney. We are still getting everything in order for us to leave in ten days. We also want to make sure everyone is well rested. Mary Barney ask us if we know of a doctor. Willie laugh a little and said that he is the town doctor. She said that she as not been feeling well for the last couple of days. Willie took her into office and givin her symptoms Willie said that he believed that she was pregnant. Mary went and told her husband Richard. They were both happy to hear the news.

1 May 1859

I am Nancy McFate,my husband Willie and I Live in Virgin,Ut with our four children. The twins, William and James then is Zion, he was born the day the first pioneers came to the Salt Lake Valley then come Leah. She is the last for now. Last year we were ask to go help settle Virgin were we live. Last week we go a letter asking us to go about a mile or so away to help settle a town which will be named Grafton,Ut with four other families. We know the Platts and the Davies. Our children are happy that they are coming they got to know the Platts and Davies well. We are going to went until The Shirts and Barneys get to Virgin to held out with Brother Tenney who is going to show us were we are going.

Willie is a doctor and as an office in Virgin which he will keep open and go there daily because most of his patients live in Virgin. He hopes our old horse can do all that work everyday. Jack Davies and Frank Platt have been called to be head of planting the cotton and wheat with the help of the other brethren.Right now Willie is the only one that as a day job that he can not leave.

We were told that the other families will be here at the end of the month with Brother Tenney. The other families are bring down te wheat and cotton form Salt Lake. which will gives us enough time to get all our homes and land sold. We do not what to leave Virgin. We have loved it there but we will still see everyone we knew when we come to town to go to the store or church.
